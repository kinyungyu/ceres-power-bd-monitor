🔍 SOFC Job Signal Decoder — Licence vs Own-Build

Not all SOFC hiring is equal. The job titles tell you whether a company is preparing to receive a Ceres licence (setting up a factory to build someone else's design) or developing proprietary technology (inventing their own). Use this framework to filter signals.

LICENCE SIGNAL — "Building to someone else's design"
Job Title to WatchWhat They're Doing
Manufacturing/Production Engineer — Fuel Cell Setting up a production line using provided recipes
Process Technician — Ceramic/Coating Operating equipment to licensor's specifications
Quality Engineer — Fuel Cell QA'ing cells against licensor's acceptance criteria
Supply Chain Manager — Ceramics/Specialty Materials Sourcing CGO powder, steel substrates, inks to spec
Systems Integration Engineer Wrapping licensed stacks into their own products
Programme Manager — Technology Transfer Managing multi-year tech transfer from licensor
Factory/Plant Manager — New Facility Running a new dedicated stack production line
Applications Engineer — SOFC/SOEC Adapting licensed tech for specific end markets
OWN-BUILD SIGNAL — "Inventing their own technology"
Job Title to WatchWhat They're Doing
Research Scientist — Electrochemistry Doing fundamental cell science from scratch
Materials Scientist — Ceramics/Perovskite Inventing new cell compositions
Cell Designer/Architect Designing their own anode/electrolyte/cathode stack
SOFC R&D Engineer (emphasis on "R&D") Experimenting, not manufacturing
Patent Engineer — Fuel Cell Filing their own proprietary IP
Lead Engineer — Thermal Spray Development Inventing deposition methods (vs using a licensor's)
Postdoctoral Researcher — SOFC Academic-style research, years from production
— All 7 GE Vernova roles fall in this column
Critical Insight — Partners Don't Need SOFC Engineers: Every existing Ceres partner (Weichai, Delta, DENSO, Thermax) signed with zero SOFC engineers on staff. Ceres' tech transfer team embeds with the partner and retrains their existing manufacturing workers. A DENSO ceramic coating technician retrains on SOFC electrolyte deposition. A Thermax process engineer learns SOFC balance-of-plant. The "golden ticket" SOFC job posting at a partner may never appear. The deal announcement will most likely come from Ceres management language on earnings calls ("imminent", "going well", "advanced discussions") — the same phrase that preceded the Delta deal by 2 months.
Revised Signal Hierarchy (Highest → Lowest Value):
  1. Ceres management commentary: "imminent licensee", "going well", "advanced discussions" on earnings calls — 1-3 month lead
  2. Ceres posts "Lead Engineer — Tech Transfer — [New Region]": deal signed, tech transfer starting — confirmation signal
  3. Analyst commentary: UBS/Goldman/Jefferies noting pipeline conversion — informed view
  4. Partner posts generic "new product line" manufacturing roles: possible but disguised, hard to catch
  5. Partner posts explicit "SOFC engineer": actually a weaker Ceres signal — more likely own-build (GE Vernova pattern)
Watch Ceres' Own Job Board: Ceres currently has a "Lead Engineer — Tech Transfer — Japan" posting (for the DENSO deal). A new "Lead Engineer — Tech Transfer — US/North America" on Ceres' career site means a US deal is signed or imminent. This is the earliest confirmation signal on the Ceres side.
